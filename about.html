About me. Alexander Kessler

My name is Andrés Alexander Cedillo Chincoya, I am 27 years old, I am a programmer and creative technologist, from Mexico City. I have a degree in Audiovisual Communication from the University of the Cloister of Sor Juana.I have specialized in electronic art and creative code, exploring topics such as computer vision, sound art, electronic literature, generative art, and machine learning. I have taken courses, workshops and diplomas such as: Introduction to the creation of electronic music (Centro ADM 2009), City Listeners Workshop (Festival of sound artifacts FONOTECA NACIONAL 2014), Introduction to interactive electronic interfaces (SAE 2015), Diploma in Media Training Electronic (Border Cultural Center 2016), Virtual reality workshop in Unity3D (CENART Multimedia Center 2017), Development of augmented reality applications (CENART Multimedia Center 2017), Sensors, Arduino and Processing Module of the Diploma in Sound Creation and New Technologies (CMMAS 2017), Module Integral Project of Max 7 of the Diploma in Sound Creation and New Technologies (CMMAS 2017), Machine Learning for Artist (CIID Summer School Costa Rica 2018), Electronic Literature (Institute of Philological Research, UNAM 2018), Reality Applications virtual in Unreal Engine (Centro de Cultura Digital CCD 2018). Photogrametry Pointclouds (TouchDesigner Summit 2019), GLSL 102: Beyond ShaderToy (TouchDesigner Summit 2019), How to create Kickass Generative Visual and not Die Trying (TouchDesigner Summit 2019) Notch Essentials (Cocolab 2020), Notch Intermediate (Cocolab 2020).

I have 5 years of experience in the entertainment industry, new technologies and interactive design. I have worked and collaborated in multimedia projects for different companies and studios such as Cultus Innovación, Altered Vjs, Siete Media, Intus Interactive Design, Surface, CLSL_LAB, I have been part of artistic pieces presented at festivals such as Mutek México, Sonar México, I have participated in the creation of software for music shows like Camilo Séptimo and DLD, Nicola Cruz & Fidel Eljuri.

During this time I have been involved in the development and creation of complex interaction systems, to communicate different media and supports in real time, such as lighting installations, tracking systems, generative visuals, sensors, alternative projection supports, kinetic lights, lasers, machine learning, webapps, VR and AR. I have been close to programming languages ​​and environments such as Javascript, Python, Openframeworks, Max / Msp / Jitter, Arduino, Processing, C #, TouchDesigner, Unity 3D, Unreal Engine, Notch VFX.

About me. Juan Manuel Piña

Juan Manuel Piña Velazquez is a multimedia artist from the State of Mexico. Graduated in Audiovisual Communication from the University of the Cloister of Sor Juana. His interests address the creation of narratives through the use of alternative and traditional media, as well as the convergence between plastic and digital disciplines in conjunction with new technologies.

With 5 years of experience within the creative, cultural and content industry, he has been immersed in the creation and development of different artistic and cultural projects, within the branches of show production and operation, 2D and 3D animation, programming interactive for VR / AR installations and experiences, as well as conceptualization, and 3D design.

He participated as a finalist in the FIMG 2015 (Girona International Mapping Festival), in Gerona, Spain in the micromapping category.

As a collaborator at Cocay Studio, he has participated in festivals such as Mutek México and Casa Mutek in the creation of artistic installations; in interactive museum projects for Museo de la Ballena, Universum, MIDE (Museo Interactivo de Economía), Ex Convento del Carmen in Guadalajara and Museo del Caracol in Ensenada, B.C .; in addition to private projects for Hannover Messe in Germany, IPSOS, Amazon Prime Mexico, Comex, among others.

As an independent artist, director and multimedia producer, he has collaborated and developed multiple projects, such as the creation of audiovisual content for the Museo del Estanquillo, in addition to collaborating with Nicola Cruz & Fidel Eljuri in the programming for their audiovisual performance presented by Boiler Room

He has experience in different digital creation environments such as Unreal Engine, TouchDesigner, Cinema4D, Adobe Suite, NotchVFX, Unity, in addition to studies in 16mm cinematography by CUEC, sculpture and drawing taught at the Academia de San Carlos, and master classes on direction and script writing taught by Guillermo Arriaga.
